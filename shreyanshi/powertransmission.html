India is set to invest more than Rs 9 lakh crore in power transmission infrastructure by 2032. 

India is set to invest Rs 9.12 lakh crore to enhance its power transmission infrastructure by 2032, according to Union Minister Shripad Yesso Naik. This expenditure is outlined in the National Electricity Plan (Transmission), which covers the period until 2031-32.

Additionally, 33.25 GW of High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) bi-pole links are planned for 2022-23 and 2031-32. The inter-regional transmission capacity is set to rise significantly, from the current 119 GW to 143 GW by 2026-27 and further to 168 GW by 2031-32.

National Electricity Plan aims to add 1,91,474 circuit kilometers of transmission lines and 1274 GVA of transformation capacity, specifically for voltage levels of 220 kV and above, between 2022-23 and 2031-32.
