Installed Capacity of Power Utilities - Dec24 

At the lower end, states like Nagaland (0.21 GW), and Chandigarh (0.25 GW) have very small installed capacities, highlighting limited power infrastructure. In contrast, states such as Gujarat (56.45 GW), Maharashtra (50.89 GW), and Rajasthan (48.56 GW) lead with the highest capacities, reflecting their larger populations, industrial growth, and energy demands.

Other notable states include Tamil Nadu (41.74 GW) and Karnataka (33.70 GW), both of which also have Substantial Power Capacities, supporting their Industrial and Urban Sectors. Uttar Pradesh (33.05 GW) and Madhya Pradesh (28.09 GW) follow closely with large installations. States like Andhra Pradesh (27.47 GW) and Telangana (19.60 GW) contribute significantly to India’s Power Grid, while smaller states like Meghalaya (0.65 GW) and Sikkim (0.77 GW) have more modest capacities.  

India’s power infrastructure shows significant regional diversity. States such as Gujarat (56.45 GW), Maharashtra (50.89 GW), and Rajasthan (48.56 GW) have the highest installed capacities, driven by large populations and industrial growth.
